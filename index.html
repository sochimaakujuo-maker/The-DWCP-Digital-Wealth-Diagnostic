The Problem

You studied something. You have developed certain skills — through courses, through work, through your own effort. You have a sense of what you want to build. And somewhere between all three of those things and actual income, there is a disconnect you have not been able to name precisely. You are not lazy. You are not behind. Your five areas are pulling in different directions.

Why It Keeps Compounding

When one area is off, you feel it — in how you answer "what do you do?", in the course that sits unfinished on your laptop, in the January plan that went quiet by March. When multiple areas are off, the friction is everywhere and you cannot see which one is actually driving the problem. You keep working. The gap stays. The five areas are:

  • 1
    What you studiedYour degree or training — and whether it is an asset or a weight in the direction you want to move.
  • 2
    What you genuinely wantThe work that gives you energy — not what is safe, not what your parents want, what you actually want to build.
  • 3
    What you are naturally good atThe things you do with unusual ease that others find difficult — your real edge, not your CV edge.
  • 4
    What skills you have builtThe capabilities you have deliberately developed — and whether they translate to income where you are building.
  • 5
    What the economy actually pays forThe specific problems people and businesses with real money will pay to have solved — right now, in your context.
